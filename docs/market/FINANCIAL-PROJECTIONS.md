## Part 4: Unit Economics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**By Channel:**

| **Channel** | **CAC** | **Volume** | **Notes** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic/Referral | $0-500 | 20% of customers | Lowest cost, high LTV |
| Self-serve (ads) | $500-1.5K | 30% of customers | Lower CAC, lower LTV |
| Sales-driven (leads) | $2-5K | 35% of customers | Higher CAC, higher LTV |
| Enterprise sales | $5-20K | 15% of customers | Highest CAC, highest LTV |
| **Blended** | **$1.5-2.5K** | **100%** | **Average across all** |

**CAC Payback Period:**
- Starter: 8-12 months
- Professional: 3-6 months
- Enterprise: 6-12 months

## Part 11: Risk Factors & Sensitivity Analysis

### Sensitivity: Impact of 50% Lower CAC

**If we achieve 50% lower CAC ($1K instead of $2K):**
- Customer acquisition cost-effective even faster
- Break-even moves up 1 month (October instead of November)
- LTV:CAC ratio improves to 40:1
- More aggressive marketing investment viable

---

### Sensitivity: Impact of 50% Higher Churn

**If monthly churn rises to 7.5% (from 5%):**
- Year 2 revenue drops to $8.5M (vs. $11.76M)
- Year 2 net income drops to $3M (vs. $4.6M)
- Still profitable, but slower growth
- Indicates product-market fit issues (needs addressing)

---

### Sensitivity: Impact of Lower ACV

**If average ACV drops 20% (to $14.4K from $18K):**
- Year 1 ARR drops to $2.0M (vs. $2.52M)
- Year 1 profitability delayed to December (vs. November)
- Year 2 ARR drops to $9.4M (vs. $11.76M)
- Still profitable, but margin-constrained
