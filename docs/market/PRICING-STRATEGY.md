## Executive Summary

Basset Hound uses a **three-tier SaaS pricing model** with clear market segmentation:
- **Starter:** $299/month (small teams, 10 monitors)
- **Professional:** $999/month (mid-market, 50 monitors)
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing (1000+ monitors, full integrations)

**Financial Projections:**
- Year 1 Target: $3-5M ARR, 50-150 customers
- Year 2 Target: $10-15M ARR, 500-1500 customers
- Gross Margin: 70%+ (SaaS standard)
- Customer Acquisition Cost: $2-5K
- Lifetime Value: $50-150K

---

## Part 1: Pricing Model Philosophy

### Core Principles

1. **Value-Based Pricing** (Not Cost-Based)
   - Price based on customer ROI, not development cost
   - Enterprise customer: 1 decision made 1 day faster = $500K+ value
   - Price should capture 10-20% of customer value created

2. **Simplicity Over Perfection**
   - Three clear tiers, not 10
   - Easy to explain in 30 seconds
   - Transparent, no hidden fees

3. **Expand Revenue with Value**
   - Start with Starter tier (low commitment)
   - Expand as customers realize value
   - Enterprise deals based on custom ROI

4. **Align with Customer Segment**
   - Small teams (startups): Starter tier
   - Mid-market (100-5000 employees): Professional tier
   - Enterprise (5000+ employees): Custom pricing

## Part 3: Pricing Anchoring & Psychology

### Price Points Rationale

**Starter at $299:**
- Below perceived cost of one person-month of work
- No internal approval needed
- Easy "trial" commitment
- Aligns with Slack Starter ($2/user = $20-50/month)

**Professional at $999:**
- 3.3x Starter (good anchoring)
- Within mid-market IT budget ($50K-100K annual)
- Requires approval but not extensive
- Comparable to Brandwatch ($500/month), positioning as premium

**Enterprise:**
- 10-50x Starter based on value
- Negotiated based on customer value creation
- Aligns with enterprise software pricing ($100K+)
- Custom creates perception of flexibility

### Psychological Pricing Elements

1. **Value Anchoring**
   - "See 1 competitor move 1 day early = $500K value"
   - ROI calculator: Input customer metrics → custom price
   - "3-6 month payback"

2. **Tier Positioning**
   - Starter: "Get started" (low risk)
   - Professional: "Recommended for most teams" (best value)
   - Enterprise: "Unlimited scale, dedicated support" (premium)

3. **Feature Naming**
   - Not "monitors" but "concurrent monitors" (sounds bigger)
   - Not "API calls" but "monthly AI-powered detections"
   - Not "support" but "dedicated intelligence analyst"

## Part 10: Pricing Go-to-Market

### Sales Pitch Structure

**30-Second Elevator Pitch:**
"Basset Hound detects competitor moves in minutes instead of hours. We start at $299/month for small teams and scale to enterprise. Customers typically see 3-6x ROI in the first year."

**Discovery Questions (Before Pricing):**
1. "How many competitors are you monitoring?"
2. "How much does it cost you to miss competitive moves?"
3. "What's your team size?"
4. "Which tools are critical to your workflow?"

**Positioning by Response:**
- 5-20 competitors, startup → Starter ($299)
- 20-50 competitors, mid-market → Professional ($999)
- 50+ competitors, enterprise → Enterprise (custom)

### Free Trial Conversion Strategy

**Starter Tier Free Trial:**
1. Pre-populate 5 sample competitors
2. Send 2-3 sample alerts (pre-recorded)
3. Day 7: Email with ROI calculator
4. Day 10: "Upgrade to Professional" offer (20% off)
5. Day 14: "Your trial expires" reminder

**Conversion target:** 10-15% → $299-999 paid tier

### Enterprise Sales Process

1. **Discovery Call (Week 1):** Understand use case, budget, timeline
2. **Technical Spike (Week 2):** POC with real competitor data
3. **ROI Presentation (Week 3):** Custom ROI calculator, proposal
4. **Negotiation (Week 4-5):** Volume discounts, terms, SLA
5. **Closing (Week 6-8):** Contract signature, onboarding

**Typical Enterprise Deal Timeline:** 60-90 days
